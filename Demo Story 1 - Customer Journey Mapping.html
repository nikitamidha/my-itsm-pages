ITForce · Customer Journey · Story 1 (proactive)

Proactive CCTV resolution — fixed before the customer knows

Atlas Logistics — Meridian cold‑storage facility. At 02:13am, Camera C‑07 in the Controlled Substances Cage is online but has stopped writing footage — a compliance failure hidden behind a green status light. Nobody reports it. TechGuard detects, diagnoses and fixes it remotely overnight, then hands the CSA a finished incident with a customer‑ready communication by morning. P1 (compliance‑critical zone). This is the proactive mirror of Story 2.

The story arc — what ran, in order, and the handoffs

1Monitor & detect
Diagnostic Agent → Intake Agent
Diagnostic Agent runs on a fixed schedule — every 5 minutes — checking footage integrity, not just heartbeat, across every Atlas camera. It catches C‑07 online with PoE (Power over Ethernet — power and network data over one cable) normal, but its last footage write is 11 minutes stale. On finding the anomaly it hands the issue to the Intake Agent to log and scope. Autonomous
⚠The operational trap: uptime is green, compliance is red. Legacy monitoring watches device heartbeat and would see nothing wrong. ITForce monitors whether footage is actually committing to storage — which is the thing the customer's audit depends on.
↓ hands the detected anomaly to the Intake Agent to log & scope
2Triage & scope
Intake Agent
Intake Agent opens the structured incident — starting the SLA + footage‑gap clocks from 02:02am — then triages and scopes it: identifies C‑07 as covering the Controlled Substances Cage, a compliance‑critical zone, and marks it P1 even though the device heartbeat is green. It then hands back to the Diagnostic Agent. Autonomous
↓ hands back to the Diagnostic Agent to troubleshoot
3Troubleshoot
Diagnostic Agent
Diagnostic Agent eliminates causes — PoE draw, network, NVR disk health, storage queue, other cameras on the same NVR. Only C‑07 is affected: its recording stream got stuck during a scheduled retention‑policy rotation. Confidence: 93%. Autonomous
↓ hands off a 93%‑confidence root cause to resolution
4Run remote fix
Resolution Agent
Resolution Agent runs the approved Recording Stream Recovery runbook — clears the stale stream lock, restarts the recording binding for C‑07, forces a new segment and reattaches the retention policy. It touches only C‑07, never the NVR or other compliance cameras. Autonomous
↓ hands off a completed runbook to verification
5Verify recovery
Resolution Agent
Resolution Agent does not self‑close. It watches new footage write at 02:25, 02:30, 02:35 and 02:40, confirms the timestamp stays under 5 minutes old, and marks recording continuity restored. Autonomous
↓ hands off a verified recovery + footage‑gap evidence to the Communications Agent
6Draft security communication
Communications Agent
Communications Agent drafts the customer notification — incident timing, root cause, resolution, time to resolution, the 02:02–02:25 footage gap (23 min), and redundancy coverage from camera C‑08. Because footage‑gap disclosure is compliance‑sensitive, it queues for CSA approval rather than sending. Autonomous
↓ hands off a drafted customer notification for approval
7Morning review, approve & send
Sarah Rose, Customer Service Agent
At 08:05am Sarah Rose logs in to a resolved overnight incident — not an emergency. She reviews the root cause, time to resolution, footage gap and redundancy note, then approves the draft, sends it to Atlas, and closes the incident. Approval Verify & Close
✓Why a human here. All outbound B2B client communications are Approval mode, and footage‑gap disclosure carries compliance exposure — the agent can't self‑send. Sarah owns the customer trust moment and the sign‑off that lets the incident close.
↓ sends the approved disclosure to the customer
8Customer receives the communication
Tom Avery, Atlas Logistics
At 08:12am Tom Avery opens the TechGuard portal and finds a communication from the ITForce system waiting in his notification center — sent by the Communications Agent, approved by Sarah. He reads the 02:02–02:25 footage gap (23 min), the root cause, the fix, and the C‑08 redundancy note. He never filed a ticket and never noticed the failure — the audit‑grade disclosure simply arrived. Notified
✓Why the customer is an actor here. Proactive resolution only lands as trust if the invisible work is surfaced. Tom’s touchpoint is the morning communication — the moment a fix nobody saw becomes a documented, compliance‑ready disclosure in his hands.
↓ hands off a closed, communicated incident to prevention
9Post‑fix prevention & self‑learning
Diagnostic Agent
Diagnostic Agent turns the incident into a forward signal — recommends checking the retention‑policy rotation on the other 5 controlled‑zone cameras before the next scheduled rotation, surfaced on the account health panel. The full incident trace — what was observed, tried and verified — feeds the agent learning loop so detection and resolution sharpen over time. Autonomous

The human in the loop — and why only one

In Story 2 a human entered three times — On‑site, Approval, and Watch. Here, the entire incident is detected, fixed and verified autonomously overnight. A human enters exactly once, in the morning, and only for the moment that needs human judgment: the compliance‑sensitive customer communication.

Why the agents were allowed to act alone
Governance logic
  • No On‑site (Ray Oak): the fix was achievable remotely — no physical fault, so no dispatch.
  • No Approval gate on the fix (Marcus Chen): the runbook was reversible and limited to a single camera stream — low blast radius — so the Resolution Agent acted autonomously.
  • The only gate is the communication, because that is where compliance exposure and customer trust live.
